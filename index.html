Hello, I'm Francis Obinna Madubuike

Dr. Obinna C. Madubuike's research primarily focuses on the application of digital twin technology in the construction and healthcare facility management sectors. Across his studies, Madubuike examines how digital twins—virtual representations of physical assets that receive real-time data from sensors and connected systems—can improve the monitoring, operation, and maintenance of buildings and infrastructure. His research highlights the limitations of traditional facility management approaches, which often rely on reactive “fix-when-broken” maintenance, and proposes digital twin–based frameworks that integrate technologies such as Building Information Modeling (BIM), IoT sensors, and data analytics to enable real-time monitoring, predictive maintenance, and better decision-making throughout the lifecycle of facilities.

The articles also include systematic and bibliometric reviews of digital twin applications in construction and the built environment, identifying current research trends, benefits, and gaps, and outlining scenarios and architectures for deploying digital twin systems in healthcare facilities and other constructed assets to improve efficiency, reliability, and asset management.
